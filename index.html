9/25/25

by:Mannix Muir

There is an often touted phrase in many comedy circles, "Get your money up, get your funny up". But this intrigued me, does more money equal more funny. I took to the comedy scene to find out the truth. When ordered by anual income comedians who earned far below average recieved an amount of cpms (chuckles per minute) similar to those who earned far above average. It turns out the closer you get to the average the less funny you actually get, as you can see in my totally real graph.

Some have begun to question my totally real and accurate data, saying things such as "hahas aren't a unit of measurement" to which I say why is it in this table then hmmmm?

Regardless of whether or not you agree with me you can tell that there is a clear link between money and funny. Whether its a bell curve, or a piano curve, or a ball curve, or what have you.
